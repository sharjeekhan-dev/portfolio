Born in the village of Noor Pur, Pakpattan — a place far from boardrooms and business schools — Sharjeel's story began not with privilege, but with purpose.

He started with a salary, no support system, and a vision that others couldn't yet see. For five years, he worked through the grind of real operations — learning finance, systems, and leadership not from textbooks, but from the floor of restaurants, warehouses, and businesses.

He left formal studies in Sahiwal and Lahore to pursue something more urgent: building. That choice, though unconventional, led to him managing multi-million rupee operations, restructuring struggling businesses, and now leading finance operations for a UK-based hospitality group.

Village roots gave me cultural values. Years of struggle gave me systems thinking. Together, they built something no degree could have.

Honesty Statement

"I have not generated personal revenue from my own businesses yet. My strength lies in delivering real operational results and growth."
